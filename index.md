# Project Overview

## Project Question

The goal of this project was to see whether pre-class videos should be required rather than optional in COMP110. 

My idea was that making pre-class required videos would introduce students to new topics before lecture and help ensure that students come to class more familiar and comfortable with the material.

---

## Analysis Summary

To explore this, I analyzed data for the survey that is related to:

- student's support for pre-class videos (`pre_lecture_videos`)
- student's understanding of the course material (`understanding`)
- the perceived course difficulty (`difficulty`)

I wanted to see whether students who support pre-class videos actually show higher understanding or lower difficulty, which would support the idea of making them required.

## Conclusion

Based on my analysis, the data does not very strongly support the idea that pre-class videos improve student understanding directly. Many students showed high amounts of support for pre-class videos, the visualizations I made didnt show a very obvious positive relationship between support for these videos and higher understanding. Actually, the comparison between high support and low support groups suggest that students who report higher support for pre-class videos do not necessarily have any higher understanding. Plus, the relationship between pre-class video support and perceived difficulty shows that students who find the course more difficult tend to have stronger support for pre-class videos. This implies that potentially rather than improving understanding directly, pre-class videos may actually be especially helpful for students who are struggling with the class material. These students most likely see pre-class videos as a helpful resource to prepare before class. A potential downside of implementing pre-class videos to be required is the additional time and effort needed from some students who dont necessarily need this resource. In the future, this idea could be further looked into by collecting data on the actual usage of pre-class videos rather than just student opinions, because some students may rate it lower, but havent actually watched them. It would also be useful to collect data on their performance post-video, like maybe a quiz after watching it. Another extension that I think could be extremely beneficial for this would be analyzing whether pre-class videos are particularly beneficial for specific groups of students (those with less prior experience in programming vs more experience). While the data does not have very strong support the original hypothesis, it does show that pre-class videos could still have value, especially for students who find the course more difficult.
