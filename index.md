# Project Overview

## Project Question

The goal of this project was to evaluate whether pre-class videos should be required rather than optional in COMP110. 

My idea was that requiring pre-class videos would introduce students to new topics before lecture and help ensure that students come to class more familiar and comfortable with the material.

---

## Analysis Summary

To explore this idea, I analyzed survey data related to:

- student support for pre-class videos (`pre_lecture_videos`)
- student understanding of course material (`understanding`)
- perceived course difficulty (`difficulty`)

The goal was to determine whether students who support pre-class videos actually show higher understanding or lower difficulty, which would support making them required.

## Conclusion

Based on my analysis, the data does not strongly support the idea that requiring pre-class videos would improve understanding for all students. While many students express high support for pre-class videos, there is no clear evidence that students who support them actually have higher levels of understanding.

Additionally, the data suggests that students who find the course more difficult are more likely to support pre-class videos. This indicates that pre-class videos may be especially helpful for students who are struggling, rather than universally beneficial for all students.

Because of this, requiring pre-class videos for all students may not be the most effective approach. A required system could introduce unnecessary workload for students who already feel comfortable with the material, without significantly improving their understanding.

A better approach may be to keep pre-class videos optional but encourage or recommend them for students who are struggling or need additional support. This allows the course to provide value where it is most needed without adding unnecessary requirements for all students.

In the future, this idea could be explored further by collecting data on actual video usage rather than just student opinions. It would also be useful to measure performance outcomes, such as quiz or assignment scores, to determine whether watching pre-class videos leads to improved learning. Another useful extension would be to analyze whether pre-class videos are particularly beneficial for students with less prior programming experience.

Overall, while requiring pre-class videos is not strongly supported by the data, the analysis suggests that they still provide meaningful value, especially for students who find the course more difficult.
